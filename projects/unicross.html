Unicross

Accessible cross-gantry 3D-printing.

Unicross was initially created because I wanted to explore the cross-gantry motion system. Cross-gantry printing is highly reliable but often expensive, and many of the existing designs using the tech are on the upper end of DIY printing performance.

Making use of low-cost linear rods, commonly-available components, and 3D printed parts, Unicross is able to drastically undercut similar machines in price while retaining immense reliability and versatility. It is capable of printing TPU, nylon, and other advanced materials with an extremely low failure rate. Full documentation is available for those who wish to replicate the machine.

Designing a machine like this came with many challenges. Cross-gantry kinematics require almost perfectly-square motion components. To cut costs and ensure self-replication, the Z axis has to be driven by a belt. The electronics have to fit in a 20mm-tall space. The result, however, is a remarkably clean and reliable machine for the price.
